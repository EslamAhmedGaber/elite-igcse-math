## How to record (3 paths, pick the easiest one)

### Path 1 — Phone screen-recording (fastest, recommended)

1. Make sure `promo.html` is on your phone. Easiest: open https://eliteigcse.com/promo.html on Safari/Chrome on your iPhone after the next push goes live (~1 min).
2. Hold phone in **portrait** orientation.
3. Pull down Control Center → tap **Screen Recording** (red dot button) → starts in 3 seconds.
4. Open Safari → tap reload on `promo.html` so the animation restarts → wait the full 28 seconds.
5. Stop recording from Control Center.
6. Open the recording in Photos → trim head and tail so only the 28-second animation remains.

### Path 2 — Desktop screen-recording (cleaner output, no status bar)

1. Open `promo.html` in Chrome on your laptop.
2. Press **F11** (full-screen) → animation fills the screen at the right 9:16 ratio (it auto-letters black bars on the side).
3. Use OBS Studio (free) or ScreenToGif:
   - Set capture region to the central 9:16 frame (Chrome will draw it automatically; you crop to it)
   - Record → reload page (Ctrl-R) so animation restarts → wait 28s → stop
4. Output is a clean MP4 with no phone status bar.

### Path 3 — Use any screen recorder (CapCut, Camtasia, Loom)

Same idea. Open `promo.html`, hit reload, capture 28s.

## Adding voiceover

The HTML is silent. You add audio in CapCut (free, mobile + desktop):

1. Import the screen recording as your video clip.
2. Tap **Audio → Record** and read either the English or Arabic voiceover script below while the video plays.
3. Or pre-record the voiceover separately in iPhone Voice Memos → import as audio track.
4. Export as MP4 vertical 1080×1920.
5. Upload to TikTok directly from CapCut.

## Three production tips

1. **Don't add music louder than your voice.** TikTok's algorithm prioritises voice content. If you want music, layer at 15–20% volume max.
2. **Subtitle the English version.** ~70% of TikTok views are sound-off. CapCut's auto-caption is one tap. Position subtitles in the lower third so they don't cover the on-screen text.
3. **Post 3 versions, not 1.** Same animation, three voiceovers: Arabic for Egypt, English for Gulf, no-voiceover-just-text for sound-off scrollers. TikTok rewards posting frequency.
